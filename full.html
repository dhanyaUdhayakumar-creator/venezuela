Brief History of Venezuela

Venezuela, officially known as the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela, is a country rich in culture and natural beauty. It was originally inhabited by various indigenous peoples before Christopher Columbus arrived in 1498. Venezuela gained independence from Spain in 1821, with Simón Bolívar playing a key role in the liberation of several South American countries. Today, Venezuela is known for its diverse landscapes, from tropical beaches to the Andes Mountains and the Amazon rainforest.

Facts about Venezuela

Why is Venezuela special?

Venezuela is known for its incredible natural diversity, from the towering Angel Falls—the world’s highest waterfall—to the unique tabletop mountains called tepuis, vibrant Caribbean beaches, and vast Amazon rainforest. It’s famous for its rich culture, music, and warm, welcoming people.

How old is Venezuela?

Venezuela, officially the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela, was founded as a nation in 1811. The land itself has a history dating back thousands of years with indigenous civilizations and colonial influences, now a dynamic country with diverse ecosystems and cultural heritage.

What is Venezuela’s slogan?

Venezuela’s official motto is “¡Independencia y patria socialismo!” which translates to “Independence and homeland socialism.” Its national colors—yellow, blue, and red—represent wealth, the sea, and the blood of independence heroes.

Venezuela: What to Do, What to See in this Vibrant Country

Ah, Venezuela! A land full of contrasts and wonders, where nature meets culture in breathtaking ways. From the towering Angel Falls to the mystical tepuis, from lively Caribbean beaches to the lush Amazon jungle, Venezuela offers adventures for every kind of traveler. It’s a country where you can lose yourself in rich traditions, vibrant music, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality. Whether exploring colonial towns, hiking through pristine national parks, or diving into the vibrant local festivals, Venezuela invites you to discover beauty, passion, and life in its purest form.

Traditional Foods from Venezuela

Arepas (Caracas and Nationwide)

Arepas are cornmeal patties that are grilled, baked, or fried and stuffed with various fillings like cheese, shredded beef, or black beans. A staple Venezuelan dish found everywhere.

Guayanés Cheese (Angel Falls Region)

A fresh, white cheese typical in the Guayana region near Angel Falls, often enjoyed with cassava bread or in arepas.

Fried Fish (Morrocoy National Park)

Freshly caught fish from the Caribbean coast, usually fried and served with coconut rice and salad — perfect after a beach day.

Casabe (Roraima Region)

Traditional cassava flatbread made by indigenous groups around the Roraima mountain area.

Patacones (Los Roques Archipelago)

Fried green plantains served as a side or snack, often accompanied by seafood dishes in the islands.

Carupanero Dulce (Medanos de Coro)

A sweet made from the carupan fruit, typical of the arid region around Coro, enjoyed as a traditional dessert.
